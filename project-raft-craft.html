RaftCraft

A chaotic multiplayer First-Person Survival game where physics-based rowing is your only hope of escape.

Active Development

Currently in the prototyping phase, testing multiplayer mechanics and procedural generation with a live playtest group.

RaftCraft is a co-op survival adventure built in Godot Engine. You wake up in a hostile jungle with a savage tribe on your trail. Your team's only escape is a raft—but navigation isn't a simple button press. It's a physics-driven struggle where players must physically move to rowing positions and coordinate torque impulses to steer the raft down a treacherous, procedurally generated river.

Core Mechanics & Tech

  • Physics-Based Navigation:

    No standard WASD steering. The raft moves based on torque applied by players rowing at specific positions. I implemented a custom physics controller that calculates vectors from player input and applies forces to the raft rigidbody, requiring real teamwork to avoid obstacles.

  • Procedural River Generation:

    The river isn't a static mesh. I engineered a system that generates the river path, waves, land, and obstacle placement along a spline, ensuring every run presents new navigation challenges.

  • "Communication-First" Gameplay Loop:

    The game is designed to force communication. Systems like the Iron Nose (which breaks obstacles but shakes the raft, ragdolling unsecured players) or the Fish Rope (high risk/reward resource gathering) create situations where players must yell useful info ("Repairs needed!", "Hard left!", "Man overboard!") every 15 seconds to survive.

Procedural Animation & Physics

To heighten the immersion of a turbulent river ride, characters use Active Ragdolls and procedural animation layers. Instead of static idle loops, characters physically balance against the raft's movement, sway with the waves, and ragdoll realistically when the raft collides with heavy obstacles.

System Depth

Beyond navigation, the game features a complex interaction system where items solve specific "core problems" (Navigation, Integrity, Enemies, Resource Pressure). For example, using Clay adds armor but freezes an inventory slot, while the Rope Launcher allows for rapid teammate rescue or sharp "bootleg turn" maneuvers at the cost of player stability.
